Cheers

Mobile App Concept

Cheers in a app concept developed around the idea of a drink delivery app. There are a lot of food delivery apps and services out there that do include drinks. However, they’re from restaurants and most of the time their menu only includes pop, juice, water, and the occasional specialty drinks. Very rarely are there establishments like coffee shops, bubble tea shops, and shops that specialize with drinks on these apps. At SozenTech we wanted to test out this idea of a drink delivery app to see if it’s desired by the people.


Approach

For this project we adopted a design-sprint approach to get some initial direction in the project and to also try different ideas while gaining valuable user insight.


Role

Designer and UX researcher


Tools

Pen, paper, sticky notes, white board, and Sketch App.

Part 1 - Onward!

Building the foundation

On the first day of our sprint we wanted to build a solid foundation for the rest of our week. As a team, we started out with setting our long term goals and sprint questions for the project.


Our long term goals

Sprint Questions

We started out with a lot of Sprint Questions but we eventually narrowed it down to what we felt were the most important questions that we wanted answered by the end of the week. These questions were:
- Do people use current delivery services and apps to just buy drinks (and occasional pastries or specialty item)?
- Do people just go out just to get drinks? If so, how often?
- Do people usually buy drinks for themselves or with others?
- Does it matter to people who delivers their drinks?
- How much does it matter to people how the drinks are presented, tracked, and delivered?
- When it comes to drinks, do people expect more security when it comes to delivery?
