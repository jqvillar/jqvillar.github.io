Executive Dashboard

In our world today, data plays a large role and affects many important decisions, actions, and ideas. The oil and gas industry is an industry that is quite familiar with the concept of “big data” and “big data” is also very important to the industry. However, data may be good, but if no one can make sense of it then the data is useless.

With Critical Control Energy Services I had the pleasure of working with them to explore where they could take their vision for creating an executive dashboard that increases their clients’ productivity, profitability, and efficiency.

Part 1 - Discovery

The Business

For this project it was important for me to really get to know the business, its products, and their competitors. It was important for me to know these because it lets me know what problem they’re trying to solve, their reasoning and motivations behind their desire to create an executive dashboard, and the goals they’re trying to achieve.

Critical Control Energy Services

Critical Control Energy Services is Canada's leading oil and gas production data management specialists and a leader in cloud-based software for the oil and gas industry. They see that due to globalization with an increasingly transient workforce, enterprises are having difficulties maintaining their knowledge and are then forced to focus on their key market advantages in order to remain competitive. Through integrated offering of software, outsourced services, and optimized business processes, Critical Control solves this problem by providing enterprises with secure and cost-effective solutions for the completion of document and information intensive business processes.

Critical Control is a company that strives to provide consistent, reliable, dependable service.


The Product

Currently Critical Control currently provides cloud-based software for the oil and gas industry which provides data measurements and management solutions and services to the North American Upstream industry.


The Competition

(Due to the nature of the work this section may contain limited information.)

Looking into their competitors some of the advantages that they have over Critical Control would be that some of their products look nicer, have established reputation by being in the industry for longer, have reputations built and backed up by large and well known organizations/companies, more transparent about their process on how they’re innovating and improving their product.

Some of their competitors have advantages over them but there are aspects where Critical Control has advantages over the competitors. Some who have long standing reputations suffer from outdated practices and processes. Some that have great looking products fall short when it comes to the functionality of their products.
